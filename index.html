    Source literature
    Lyons et al. (EJVES 2026)
    MAGIC criteria validation. 188 patients, median follow-up 2.9 years. CRP ≥2 mg/L sensitivity 100% for eventual VGEI diagnosis. Suspected cases: 59% progressed to diagnosed within follow-up. Minimum 2 years follow-up required for suspected cases.
    Husmann et al. (EJNMMI 2018)
    266 PET/CT scans in 68 patients (VASGRA cohort). Stop-supportive threshold SUVmax 4.3 / CRP 3.5; continue-supportive at SUVmax 6.0 / CRP 9.95; escalate-supportive at SUVmax 9.5 / CRP 31.5. PET/CT added value even when CRP normal.
    Kouijzer et al. (CMI 2025)
    Delphi consensus on VGEI treatment and follow-up. 35 experts, 4 rounds. Empiric antibiotics only for sepsis/bleeding/delayed diagnosis. Duration guidance: 6 weeks (complete removal + autologous vein), 12 weeks (prosthetic remains), 6 months (MRSA/MDR/Candida/no surgery).

    MAGIC criteria assessment

    Enter findings to determine VGEI classification per published criteria
    MAGIC classification rules
    Definite VGEI: At least one major criterion + at least one other criterion from any different category (major or minor)
    Suspected VGEI: Isolated major criterion OR minor criteria from ≥2 categories
    Not infected: Does not meet criteria for suspected or definite VGEI

    Major criteria

    CT or MRI showing abscess cavity adjacent to graft
    Direct communication between graft and bowel or bronchus
    Focal, intense uptake at graft site (after >3 months post-op)
    e.g., treatment of infected native aortic aneurysm

    PET/CT interpretation

    Stop / continue / escalate guidance from VASGRA cohort
    Timing gate
    FDG activity may be false-positive less than 3 months post-surgery. Inhomogeneous uptake is frequent in non-infected polyester grafts. PTFE grafts only show focal uptake in true infection.
