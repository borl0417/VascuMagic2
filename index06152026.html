Evidence summary

Evidence base
[1] Lyons et al., EJVES 2026 — "Outcomes of Aortic Graft and Endograft Infection Using MAGIC Diagnostic Criteria: Recommendations for Follow Up." 188 patients prospectively followed. Key findings: 59% of suspected cases progressed to diagnosed VGEI within 2 years; CRP ≥2 mg/L had 100% sensitivity; normal WBC (sensitivity 51%) cannot exclude VGEI; minimum 2 years follow-up recommended.
[2] Husmann et al., EJNMMI 2018 — "The role of FDG PET/CT in therapy control of aortic graft infection." 266 PET/CT scans in 68 patients (VASGRA cohort). Key findings: stop-supportive at SUVmax ~4.3 / CRP ~3.5; continue-supportive at SUVmax ~6.0 / CRP ~9.95; escalate-supportive at SUVmax ~9.5 / CRP ~31.5. PET/CT added value even when CRP was normal. Timing gate: >3 months post-surgery required.
[3] Kouijzer et al., CMI 2025 — "Treatment and follow-up of VGEI: Delphi consensus document." 35 experts, 4 rounds. Key findings: empiric antibiotics only for sepsis/bleeding/delayed diagnosis; 6 weeks after complete removal + autologous vein; 12 weeks if prosthetic remains; 6 months for MRSA/MDR/Candida/poor source control; rifampicin contraindicated in long-term SAT; CE-CT at 1w, 3m, 6m, then annually.

Module 3 · Antibiotic Duration

Antimicrobial therapy guidance

Duration is determined by surgical extent and microbiology. Organism-based rules override material-based rules.

⏸ Cultures pending — hold antibiotic duration output Obtain blood cultures (2–3 sets) + direct samples before starting empiric therapy unless sepsis or bleeding is present. 3–5 deep intraoperative specimens recommended if proceeding to surgery.
[3] Delphi Q1–2, Table 1 items 5–6
Additional questions

Empiric coverage — by location
Abdominal VGEI without aorto-enteric fistula: Gram-positive cocci + gram-negative bacilli + anaerobes. Add MRSA/MRSE cover based on local epidemiology and risk factors. [3] Delphi Q4
Thoracic VGEI without aorto-oesophageal fistula: Gram-positive cocci. Add MRSA cover if high local prevalence. [3] Delphi Q5
With aorto-enteric / aorto-oesophageal fistula: Broad: gram-positives + gram-negatives + anaerobes + MRSA + fungi (consider GI/bronchial flora). [3] Delphi Q6 — conditional, 77% consensus
Consult infectious diseases specialist for specific antibiotic selection. Obtain cultures as soon as feasible and de-escalate.

Rifampicin — staphylococci
Rifampicin may be added for staphylococcal VGEI only after: (1) source control confirmed, (2) no active bacteraemia, (3) no upcoming surgical procedures planned. Must be combined with another antibiotic active against staphylococci to prevent resistance.
[3] Delphi Q10
⚠ Rifampicin contraindicated in long-term SAT. Rifampicin should be discontinued in the context of long-term suppressive antimicrobial therapy due to resistance development risk and toxicity. [3] Delphi Q21
CRP normalization check
Prolongation trigger: if CRP fails to normalize during follow-up, antimicrobial therapy should be prolonged regardless of the initial duration rule.

⚠ PET/CT timing gate — interpret with caution FDG activity may be false-positive in the early post-operative phase. Inhomogeneous uptake is frequent in non-infected polyester grafts.
